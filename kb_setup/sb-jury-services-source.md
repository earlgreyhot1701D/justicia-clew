## Reporting Instructions

If you have received a Trial Jury Summons, examine the entire document carefully for the following information:

* Summons Date
* Jury Group Number
* Juror ID Number
* 24 hour information phone line

You will need to check the Superior Court's online jury portal or call the court's automated phone line at 877-544-5094 after 5:30 PM the evening prior to your summons date for the reporting instructions for your group. You will need to have your Juror ID number available.

Courtrooms operate 8:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday through Friday, except holidays. Reporting time for prospective jurors is usually 8:00 or 8:30 a.m., but the court may ask you to report later in the day depending on need. Your specific reporting time still comes from the portal or the automated line, not this general window.

## Lost or Didn't Receive Your Summons?

If a juror lost or didn't receive their summons or doesn't have access to their Juror ID number, contact Jury Services directly. Office hours are 8 a.m. to 3 p.m., Monday through Friday, excluding holidays.
North County: (805) 614-6464; South County: (805) 882-4530.

## Check Jury Reporting Status

You must access the Superior Court's online jury portal or call the court's automated phone line at 877-544-5094 to check for your status as directed during your on-call period to determine if you are required to report for service.

Please have your summons handy when calling. You will need to enter your specific Juror ID Number to access your record.

You will be placed on call for approximately five (5) court days. If directed to report for jury service you MUST bring your summons with you when you report and you MUST be on time. If you are not directed to report for service during your on-call period, you will be released as instructed on the recorded message or the court's website.

## Requesting a Postponement, Excusal, or Disqualification

The Juror Access portal (accessed with your Juror ID and date of birth from your postcard summons, or by scanning the QR code on the postcard) lets you complete your juror questionnaire, opt in to text/email reminders, and submit a request to postpone, excuse, or disqualify yourself from service. If you don't have internet access, you can complete these same requests in person at the courthouse on your reporting day, or by calling the jury office directly: (805) 882-4530 for the Santa Barbara Jury Office, or (805) 614-6464 for the Santa Maria Jury Office.

## Employer Information

Every year in Santa Barbara County, public and private employees provide jurors for the Court's jury system, and the jury system cannot exist without that support.

In Santa Barbara County, the term of service is one sworn trial. California law allows the court to call jurors in for up to five call-in days. Jurors are expected to report for one day of jury selection and to accept all trials regardless of length, unless it would be a personal hardship. If you're in jury selection (voir dire) or sworn to a case, you're not limited to the five on-call days, your term ends when the trial reaches a verdict or deliberations conclude. If you're not selected or serving by the fifth day, your term ends at the close of business that day.

California Government Code section 12945.8 states, in part, that an employer shall not:

1. Discharge or in any way discriminate against an employee for taking time off to serve as required by law on an inquest jury or trial jury, if the employee gave the employer reasonable notice beforehand that they were required to serve.
2. Discharge or in any way discriminate or retaliate against an employee, including a victim, for taking time off to appear in court to comply with a subpoena or other court order as a witness in a judicial proceeding.

This is the legal basis for the common "can my employer fire me for jury duty" question. The court's page does not go further into how to file a complaint if this happens, for that a juror would need to contact the Labor Commissioner or an employment attorney, this page only confirms the underlying legal protection.

## Juror Information (general)

Your responsibility as a juror is to decide the facts and apply the law impartially, without favor or prejudice.

Court security: if you're exhibiting respiratory symptoms, shortness of breath, coughing, headache, or flu-like symptoms, do not report for jury service, contact Jury Services right away instead. All persons and property entering the courthouse are subject to search and screening. Don't bring large backpacks or bags (a small purse or clear bag is fine), and don't bring anything that could be considered a weapon (pocket knives, box cutters, nail clippers, metal tweezers, knitting needles, metal utensils, bottle cap openers, firearms), these will be confiscated and will slow down your screening.

Jury scam alert: Santa Barbara County Superior Court is aware of scams where identity thieves impersonate jury office staff and threaten people for failing to report, then ask for confidential information. Jury Services staff never ask for Social Security numbers, credit card numbers, bank account numbers, or other sensitive information over the phone. If you get a call like this, contact local law enforcement.

## Juror Parking

**Santa Barbara Courthouse:**
Limited juror parking is available at 1021 Santa Barbara Street (located between Figueroa & Carrillo Streets). Parking is extremely limited and is first-come first-served. For reporting prospective jurors, please show your summons to the parking attendant to gain entry to the lot. The jury parking lot opens at 7:30am. Parking for a fee is also available in the city parking lot at the corner of Anacapa and Figueroa streets. DO NOT PARK in any timed zone spaces as your car may be ticketed. Parking tickets will not be validated.

**Santa Maria Courthouse:**
There is limited juror parking at the courthouse. It is recommended to park in the Town Center mall parking lot on Cook Street and walk to the Jury Assembly Building F located in the rear of the courthouse complex. If driving from US 101 North, exit at Stowell Road in the right hand lane and head west on Stowell. Proceed approximately one mile to Miller Street. Turn right on Miller Street and proceed approximately one mile. Turn left on Cook Street. If driving from Lompoc, take CA-135/Broadway north to Santa Maria. Turn right on Cook Street.

**Lompoc Courthouse:**
Parking is available in the Civic Center parking lot and in the County lot located behind the courthouse. If driving from Santa Maria, proceed south on Broadway/CA-135 approximately 4.5 miles south of Orcutt. Merge right onto Highway 1 towards Vandenberg Space Force Base. Follow Highway 1 for 6 miles and turn left at Vandenberg Space Force Base main gate. Continue 6.5 miles and merge right onto H Street. Follow H Street south through Lompoc to Ocean Avenue, approximately 2.5 miles. Turn left onto Ocean Avenue to D Street. Turn right onto D Street, into the Civic Center parking lot and in the County lot behind the courthouse.
